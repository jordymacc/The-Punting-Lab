Golden Rule: If the analysis isn't clear, don't bet. Every bet should have a documented reason based on data — never gut feel, never chasing losses.
📊
VALUE BETTING
Value exists when the true probability of a horse winning is higher than what the market implies. If a horse has a 30% chance of winning, fair odds are $3.33. If you can get $4.50, that's a 35% edge per bet.
Focus on Overlay Score. Target STRONG (≥10) and GOOD (≥6) rated horses only. Skip races with no clear value.
🎯
FIXED VS PARIMUTUEL
Fixed Odds — You lock in a price before the race. Better for value hunting as the price is guaranteed regardless of late money. Use when you've identified an overlay.

Parimutuel (Tote) — Pool betting where final odds depend on all bets placed. Can offer better returns on outsiders but harder to predict exact payout.
For value betting, fixed odds are preferred. You know your edge before placing the bet.
🏇
FORM ANALYSIS
Recent form is the strongest predictor. A horse that has finished 1st or 2nd in its last two starts on similar distance and track conditions is your starting point — not the endpoint.
Check: barrier draw, jockey form at this track, days since last run (14–28 days is ideal), and track condition history.
🌦️
TRACK CONDITIONS
Track condition is massively underweighted by casual bettors. Heavy tracks produce enormous upsets. Some horses are specialist wet trackers — their price often doesn't reflect this in the morning market.
Always check condition win % before any bet. A 40%+ condition win rate on a Heavy 8 track is a significant edge.
🧠
DISCIPLINE RULES
1. Never bet more than your daily loss limit
2. Never increase stake to chase losses
3. Never bet on races with no GOOD+ overlay
4. Keep records — every bet, every result
5. Review monthly, not race by race
Emotion is your biggest opponent. The bookmaker is patient. Be more patient.
🗓️
SPECIALIST RACES
Fillies & Mares — Form can be inconsistent, but class dropbacks are very valuable.

Maiden Races — Most reliable form is recent trial/jumpout data. Lean toward small fields.

Group Races — Public overbet favourites. Look for forgotten horses with class.
📍
TRACK BIASES
Flemington — Wide, flat. Speed from barriers 1–4 dominant in sprints.

Caulfield — Tight turns favour handy horses. Inside barriers critical.

Randwick — Long straight rewards staying power. Barrier less important in middle distances.

Eagle Farm — Heavy bias to on-pace runners.
📈
CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
Review your results every 50 bets minimum. Look for: which track types returned positive ROI, which rating tiers performed best, which race types you over- or under-performed in.
Strategy evolves with data. Your edge today may not be your edge in 6 months. Adapt or lose it.
💡
Exotic bets offer larger returns but require more precision. Allocate no more than 20% of your daily budget to exotics. They are high-variance by nature.
QUINELLA
Pick two horses to finish 1st and 2nd in either order. Lower payout than exacta but much easier to win.
Best used when two horses are clearly superior to the field. Use the top 2 Fair Value runners from this tool.
Example: Horses A & B quinella
A wins, B 2nd ✅ / B wins, A 2nd ✅
EXACTA
Pick the 1st and 2nd place horses in exact finishing order. Higher payout than quinella but harder to win.
Reverse the exacta (A/B + B/A) for safety — costs double but covers both orders.
Example: Horse A 1st, Horse B 2nd
Only A wins and B places 2nd ✅
TRIFECTA
Pick 1st, 2nd, and 3rd in exact order. Large payouts possible but requires precision.
Use a "banker" — one horse you're very confident in as the winner. Then box or wheel the other two positions with 3–4 runners.
Banker wheel: Fix A in 1st
Positions 2+3: B, C, D (6 combinations)
FIRST 4
Pick the first 4 horses in exact order. Massive payouts but extremely difficult. Reserve for fields of 10+ with a clear top 4.
Box the top 5 Fair Value runners for a cost-effective approach to the First 4.
5 horses boxed = 120 combinations
Cost = 120 × unit stake
MULTI / PARLAY
Combine multiple single bets — all must win. Odds multiply but so does risk. One loss wipes the ticket.
Limit to 3-leg multis maximum. Only use STRONG or GOOD rated horses. Never include odds-on favourites — poor value.
3-leg multi at $3 × $4 × $5 = $60
$1 stake returns $60 if all win
PLACE BETTING
Horse must finish 1st, 2nd, or 3rd (in fields of 8+). Lower returns but significantly higher strike rate. Useful for building bankroll consistency.
Target horses with high Place % (50%+) and a Place fair value showing positive overlay. Lower risk, lower reward.
Career Place % + Track Place %
Both above 45% = strong place candidate
